Research Project using Python (ongoing)

There exists two starting points a,b, conducting random walks on a given interval, with its endpoints A and B respectively. Every loop, the position of point B is graphed under certain conditions. It is known that under P(a < b| A < B), the position of B is normally distributed with the mean b.

However, two new conditions are applied:
1. a < B < A or A < B < a.
2. B > 50 and b > 50 or B < 50 and b < 50

This research has implications in information security as point b may be private but point B may be public. Therefore, it can lead to outsiders having access to private information.

This is an ongoing research project mentored by Professor Vladimir Shpilrain in City College of New York.
